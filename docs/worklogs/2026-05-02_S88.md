## Context

S87 launched the SP17b convergence cohort and drafted the CoRL abstract claiming
"Nash equilibrium convergence at capture rate 0.51 ± 0.03". S88 trajectory
analysis on 2026-05-02 (`/tmp/seed_analysis/analyze.py`) revealed cohort-wide
curriculum-end drift: every SP17b seed drops capture rate by ≈0.12 at the
exact micro-phase where the masking curriculum reaches `p_full = 0` (M2550,
≈5.22 M steps). Specifically:

- **s48**: drift continues post-boundary; last 8-eval mean 0.315.
- **s49**: drift slower; mean 0.399.
- **s50**: had not yet crossed the boundary at session start.
- **s51**: pursuer success-rate dropped to 0.000 for ≥5 evals; rolled back to
  phase-900 with peak CR 0.13. Recovery without config change unlikely.

The "0.51" headline is therefore a **curriculum artifact** — measured during
late stages of curriculum decay, before `p_full = 0`. Under the deployment
condition (full PO, no curriculum aid), the cohort sits at CR ≈ 0.31–0.40.

The central question shifts from *"did self-play converge to CR=0.5?"* to
*"is the policy pair the cohort produced at (or near) a Nash equilibrium of
this game, regardless of the numerical CR value?"* This session implements
and runs the BR exploitability test specified in
`docs/superpowers/specs/2026-05-02-paper-strengthening-design.md` to answer
that — and select the paper framing (A/B/C) accordingly.
