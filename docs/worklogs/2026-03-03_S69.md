### Literature Research: Masking Curriculum for Pursuit-Evasion

Conducted comprehensive literature survey across 5 research questions:

1. **Does curriculum on observation space work?** — YES. PO-GRL (Weigand & Peters, 2021) directly validates this: smooth transition from full→partial observability improves final policy performance vs training under partial obs from the start. Tested with SAC. GPO (2025) extends this with constrained guider-learner co-training.

2. **Failure modes identified**:
   - Catastrophic forgetting when observation distribution shifts (state coverage drift)
   - Feature reliance on observations that become unavailable
   - Teacher-student gap (teacher over-relies on privileged info)
   - Policy collapse if transition is too abrupt

3. **Successful transition strategies**:
   - PO-GRL: Linear annealing of full-obs sample probability (smooth)
   - GPO: Concurrent teacher-student with backtracking constraints
   - Asymmetric actor-critic: Critic uses full obs, actor uses partial (Pinto et al., 2018)
   - CTS: Concurrent teacher-student for locomotion (2024)
   - Pursuit-evasion specific: Linear decay of sensing params over first 25% of training (MADDPG, 2024)

4. **Observation dropout alternative**: Supported by domain randomization literature. Sensor Dropout (SensD) randomly masks input features. Can be combined with curriculum via increasing dropout rate.

5. **PE-specific examples**:
   - PEBS (belief states + self-play curriculum)
   - MADDPG with FOV/range curriculum (360°→90°, 10m→7.5m)
   - Vision-based PE with teacher-student distillation (Bajcsy, 2023)
